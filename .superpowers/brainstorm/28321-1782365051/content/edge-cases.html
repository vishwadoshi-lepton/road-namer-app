Edge cases — reversed twins & broken-corridor orphans

Both grounded in the real Shillong data. The twins need a decision; the orphans just need a confirm.

① Reversed-direction twins

A physical road often appears as two routes — Route 273 and Route 273 (Reversed) — same road, opposite directions, two different uuids.

88 reversed leaves 69 with a clean forward twin 68/69 geometry-confirmed 19 lonely (named normally)
→ Route 273 ← Route 273 (Reversed) ⇒ ↔ Route 273 · one row, name once → both directions get it · 2 uuids preserved for sync

How should twins behave?

A

Auto-link confirmed twins — name once, applies to both (recommended)

Only when base-name matches and geometry confirms the reversal (so no false pairing). Shows as a single "↔ both directions" row; the name flows to both uuids on sync. An "unlink" control is there for the rare case the two directions genuinely differ. The 19 lonely reversed routes appear as normal items.

B

Treat every direction independently

No linking — the inspector names Route 273 and Route 273 (Reversed) separately. Simpler logic, but ~69 redundant entries and a real risk the same road gets two different names by accident.

② Broken-corridor orphans — confirming your rule, no decision needed

When a parent has even one unsynced child, it isn't shown as a corridor; its surviving synced children become standalone — same look as any standalone (no special color, per your call).

Each orphan keeps its imported name (e.g. Route 385 - Segment 2) pre-filled, so its origin is still legible — but it's named as its own road. Nothing references the broken parent.
